Why?

Coffee is the world's favorite beverage, but the industry faces serious sustainability and fairness issues. Built on an extraction model, it often exploits cheap labor and depletes natural resources.

When brewing a cup, we only use less than 1% of its nutritional value. We aim to change that by unlocking the full 99%. Join us in making every cup count!

Coffee Issues

The coffee industry is worth billions, yet many coffee farmers struggle to meet their basic needs. Low prices force them to operate at a loss, unable to afford healthy diets, education, or healthcare. Sustainable practices become out of reach, and younger generations are leaving the farms behind.

Environmentally, current coffee production contributes to climate change, deforestation, and biodiversity loss, with water pollution and poor waste management as serious side effects. Over 99% of the coffee plant's nutritional value is wasted, with millions of tonnes of fruit pulp polluting the soil and water.

This calls for a circular transformation in the industry, addressing both the human and environmental costs.
